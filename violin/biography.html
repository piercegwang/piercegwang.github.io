Biography

Pierce Wang is a violinist currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Violin Performance at the Juilliard School in New York, under the guidance of Catherine Cho.

A recent graduate of the Columbia-Juilliard Exchange Program, Pierce Wang holds a degree in Computer Science from Columbia University, while also studying violin at Juilliard with renowned pedagogues Hyo Kang and Adele Anthony. Passionate about computer science and problem solving, this interdisciplinary background has informed Pierce Wang’s approach to music, bringing distinct analytical perspective and creative problem-solving skills to his artistic pursuits.

Prior to his studies at Juilliard, Pierce Wang spent 12 years at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music Pre-college, where he studied with Alexander Barantschik, Alena Tsoi, Wei He, and Davis Law.

In the summer of 2024, Pierce Wang participated in the Domaine Forget de Charlevoix music festival in Quebec, where he performed with his string quartet and as a soloist. Masterclasses with distinguished pedagogues such as Mihaela Martin, Stéphanie-Marie Degand, Vilmos Szabadi, and Rachel Barton Pine provided invaluable insights and inspiration. Additionally, Pierce Wang had the privilege of working with Kerson Leong and Francesca dePasquale in private lessons.

Pierce Wang has also had the opportunity to study with other notable artists, including a summer at the Heifetz Institute in 2020, where he worked with Kyung Sun Lee, Ilya Kaler, and others. At the age of 10, Pierce Wang made his national broadcast debut on NPR’s From the Top, performing Heinrich Wilhelm Ernst’s Der Erlkönig.

As a soloist, Pierce Wang has appeared with several orchestras in the Bay Area, including Symphony Parnassus, Saratoga Symphony, and Solano Symphony, performing concertos by Prokofiev, Saint-Saëns, Conus, and Tchaikovsky. Recent solo concerts have showcased Pierce Wang’s technical mastery and musical depth, including a 2023 recital featuring all six Ysaÿe Sonatas, accompanied by personal introductions and demonstrations. Pierce’s undergraduate senior concert highlighted his interpretive range and stamina with works by CPE Bach, Beethoven, and Brahms, while a concert in Taiwan featured a more playful and diverse program of works by John Williams, Beethoven, Kreisler, Corigliano, and Bach.
